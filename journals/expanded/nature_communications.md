# Nature Communications Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Broad multidisciplinary science audience
- Editorial focus: Substantive contribution with broad scientific relevance
- Best use case: Submit when your core result is meaningful beyond a narrow subfield.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Breadth and relevance of contribution
2. Abstract clarity for non-specialist experts
3. Methods sufficiency for confidence in findings
4. Figure readability under quick review

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Test abstract on one expert outside your exact niche
- Ensure each figure answers one explicit question
- Keep methods language consistent across sections
- Add one sentence on study boundaries in discussion

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Story is technically sound but audience fit is too narrow
- Main conclusion is buried in secondary analyses
- Figures are dense and hard to interpret quickly

## Submission package checklist
- Abstract with one clear contribution sentence
- Figure-level story map before submission
- Cover letter with broad relevance framing
