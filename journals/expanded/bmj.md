# BMJ Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Clinical and public health audience
- Editorial focus: Methodological transparency and practical health relevance
- Best use case: Submit when your results have direct implications for policy or practice.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Real-world health relevance
2. Reporting transparency
3. Methods clarity for auditability
4. Balance between claim and uncertainty

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Add plain-language implication statement
- Use a reporting checklist before submission
- Make limitations concrete and specific
- Verify references tied to policy or clinical claims

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Public health relevance not clearly stated
- Methods are correct but hard to audit
- Limitations are generic instead of specific

## Submission package checklist
- Public health implication sentence
- Transparent methods and reporting checklist mapping
- Clean distinction between findings and interpretation
