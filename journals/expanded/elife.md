# eLife Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Life science researchers with strong methods expectations
- Editorial focus: Transparent and rigorous science
- Best use case: Submit when methods and data transparency are central strengths.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Method transparency and reproducibility
2. Coherence between claims and figures
3. Clarity of uncertainty and boundaries
4. Overall evidence quality

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Perform a reproducibility audit on methods and data links
- Review figure logic with one external reader
- Label exploratory findings explicitly
- Keep conclusion language proportional

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Methods under-detailed for reproducibility
- Figure logic requires too much inference
- Claims are strong but uncertainty is under-reported

## Submission package checklist
- Reproducibility and data transparency pass
- Figure narrative consistency pass
- Discussion with explicit boundaries
