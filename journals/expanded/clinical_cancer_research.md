# Clinical Cancer Research Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Oncology translational and clinical investigators
- Editorial focus: Mechanistic and clinically meaningful oncology findings
- Best use case: Submit when translational significance is explicit and evidence-backed.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Clinical or translational relevance
2. Biomarker rationale and validation depth
3. Strength of mechanistic support
4. Cohort and endpoint clarity

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Validate biomarker claims with clear evidence tiers
- Align translational language with data strength
- Ensure endpoint definitions are unambiguous
- Review oncology references for precision and currency

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Biomarker claims without adequate validation depth
- Translational claim not tied to concrete evidence
- Figure narrative does not clearly show treatment effect context

## Submission package checklist
- Translational relevance statement
- Biomarker validation summary
- Endpoint definitions and evidence map
