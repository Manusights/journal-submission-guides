# Nature Medicine Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Translational and clinical biomedical researchers
- Editorial focus: Clear mechanistic or clinical relevance with strong evidence
- Best use case: Submit when your central claim and strongest figure align clearly with this journal's readership.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Scope fit in title and abstract
2. Novelty framing in the first two paragraphs
3. Methods credibility for the core claim
4. Figure clarity for the main result

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Write one sentence that states the exact contribution, no hype
- Verify all references tied to headline claims
- Confirm methods include enough detail for external replication
- Ensure limitations are explicit and proportional to claim strength

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Incremental advance framed as a major breakthrough
- Clinical implications that exceed the actual evidence
- Weak external validation for biomarker or model claims

## Submission package checklist
- Cover letter mapped to clinical relevance
- Structured statement of cohort, endpoint, and effect size
- Data availability and ethics statements included
