# Journal of Clinical Oncology Submission Guide

## Journal profile
- Primary audience: Clinical oncology researchers and practitioners
- Editorial focus: Clinically relevant oncology evidence with robust design
- Best use case: Submit when trial design, endpoint logic, and interpretation are tightly aligned.

## What editors usually screen first
1. Clinical question importance
2. Endpoint hierarchy and analysis integrity
3. Safety reporting quality
4. Relevance for oncology practice

## Practical pre-submission checks
- Validate endpoint hierarchy across abstract, methods, and results
- Make statistical analysis plan easy to follow
- Ensure adverse event reporting is coherent across tables and text
- Reduce claims that exceed trial scope

## Common avoidable rejection patterns
- Clinical endpoint hierarchy is ambiguous
- Statistical analysis plan is hard to follow
- Interpretation extends beyond trial design limits

## Submission package checklist
- Endpoint and analysis consistency check
- Safety clarity pass on all key outputs
- Discussion rewritten to match trial scope
